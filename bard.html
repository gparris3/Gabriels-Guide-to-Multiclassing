The Bard Class

A support-heavy class in combat, a menace outside of combat. The party face 9 times out of 10, and more skill bonuses than you'll ever use.

Notable Features

Bardic Inspiration

Depending on your level, give a creature (including yourself) an extra die to use on a roll of their choosing.

Jack Of All Trades

If you aren't proficient in a skill, you can add half of your proficiency bonus anyway.

Expertise

If you are proficient in a skill, you add your proficiency bonus twice.

Possible Combinations

Barbarian

An effective all-rounder, supplementing Barbarian's hyperfocus on combat with Bard's everything else. Alternatively, if you can plan ahead, advantage on your rolls and disadvantage on your enemies' can lead to very effective grappling.

Cleric

Doubles down on support, but you have to juggle both WIS and CHA. Lore Bard can use Magical Secrets to get spells that Life Cleric wouldn't normally be able to access like Healing Spirit and apply its healing buff to those as well.

Druid

Buffs and summons and battlefield control, all in a single character. Like Cleric, you need to juggle WIS and CHA, but it can work well with some planning.

Fighter

Rough with a makeup of primarily Bard, but as always Action Surge is nice to have. College of Swords can work well, as its flourishes mesh with Battle Master's maneuvers to have several options in combat.

Monk

Works surprisingly well. Way of the Drunken Master's text description often uses dancers and performers alongside drunkards, so its flavoring can easily be narrowed down. This already matches Bard, and things like College of Swords can push this further as their martial and melee weapons can be used as a spellcasting focus, and their maneuvers can compliment Monk's dip in, dip out nature.

Paladin

Higher level spell slots for use on Divine Smite, extra slots for your lower level spells, and Find Steed for excellent maneuverability (which can very well be abused with spells like Armor of Agathys). A good combo that doesn't push your ability scores too far.

Ranger

No real synnergies here. Lots of utility, I suppose.

Rogue

Between more proficiencies, more expertise, Jack of All Trades, and Reliable Talent... If you've failed any skill check the DM is spiting you. Great 1 level dip, if you'll ever need that many skill checks.

Sorcerer

A good choice to pump up your spellcasting. No alternating ability scores, so a simple choice if you want a more active role in combat.

Warlock

Great combination, more proficiencies, and Hexblade is always nice. Use Charisma for melee attacks and gain access to the ever-useful Eldritch Blast. Medium armor and shields also boost your survivability, while making it so you'll never need more than 14 DEX.

Wizard

Tempting, but Sorcerer is better. Dependent on another ability score, so stretches you thin. Ask yourself if you can't get what you want out of this by taking Magic Initiate as a feat.

Closing Notes

Excels when it's able to support itself. Drops off if you have to use more ability scores, but a lot of casting uses Charisma anyway, so stays relevant. Excellent to pick up more skills. If you can plan ahead, you can easily pull the strings of the entire combat, fitting for a leader.

by Gabriel Parrish
