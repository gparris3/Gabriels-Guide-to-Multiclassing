Notable Features

Eldritch Invocations

Powerful options, though most are locked behind having a certain amount of Warlock levels. Great choices include Pact of the Blade, Eldritch Sight, and Agonizing Blast. Though, naturally, these will change with the build you're going for.

Pact Magic

Though not as powerful as it was in the 2014 version, Warlocks still have the same general mindset. Powerful spells, but you can't use them often. In exchange. you get them back on a short rest as well as a long rest and have some great cantrips to choose from as well.

Possible Combinations

Barbarian

Doesn't synergize too well with Warlock as the primary class. Rage restricts your spellcasting. There are some options that work, but other classes have better ones.

Bard

Another Charisma based caster. Plus, Expertise and Jack of All Trades are only 2 levels in. A decent dip if you want to pick up some out-of-combat utility and more spell slots to work with.

Cleric

Fun roleplay, tricky combat. Life Cleric and Celestial Warlock can mesh fair enough, if you want to try this combo.

Druid

Just take Circle of the Land or Eldritch Adept.

Fighter

Some great options if you pick up Fighter first. Constitution saving throw proficiency, heavy armor and shields, and a Fighting Style work very well with Hexblade and Pact of the Blade.

Monk

Monk's abilities can synergize well with Hex, Hexblade's Curse, and it won't interfere with your spellcasting. Both classes regain essential features on a short rest. Some higher levels can begin to fall off, though.

Paladin

A classic combination. Hexblade Warlock and Pact of the Blade combine with Paladin's proficiencies to make a very straightforward Charisma based martial. Hexblade's Curse gives you more raw damage, and recovering slots on a short rest gives you high level slots to funnel right into your Divine Smite. While not as powerful now that 2024's ruleset only applies the max spell level to Warlock slots, it's still a great multiclass.

Ranger

There is potential here, but it does require a specific goal in mind. Darkness and Devil's Sight can synergize well with Gloom Stalker Ranger, and a lot of Ranger's spells don't really need WIS. It has its niche, if you're set on the combo.

Rogue

A personal favorite, especially if you're good and talking your DM into interpreting things certain ways. Take Assassin Rogue and Hexblade Warlock. If you so desire, maybe even Changeling. Take Devil's Sight and Armor of Shadows as your first invocations. This is a situation where the power of Devil's Sight truly shines, as essentially the only other way to be able to see in magical darkness is Truesight. You get Advantage for free since you can cast Darkness through Warlock, and if you convince your DM you can argue that being unable to see anything counts as being Surprised, so you always crit because of Assassinate.

Sorcerer

Your DM will hate you. An infamous multiclass for one specific reason: a strategy dubbed CoffeeLock. Luckily, this was worked around in the 2024 ruleset, but previously there was a loophole which allowed you to farm sorcery points limitlessly. Still a powerful combination today, as you still regain your Warlock spells on a short rest, which you can funnel into sorcery points for incredible longevity and versatility.

Wizard

There are two options to consider here. On the primarily Wizard end of things. with 10 Wizard and 2 Warlock, combining Empowered Invocation and Agonizing Blast can allow you to add both INT and CHA to every Eldritch Blast beam for amazing DpR. Alternatively, you can combine Arcane Ward with Armor of Shadows to gain incredible survivability, and if you pick up Armor of Agathys it can just get silly.

Closing Notes

While it can't combine with everything, Warlock serves well to add powerful options to the classes it will work with.
