The Wizard Class

The ultimate utility casters, Wizards are only really limited by their spellbooks and their spell slots. They may not have much outside of their spellcasting, but they make it worth the dedication.

Notable Features

Spellcasting

Wizards are essentially the spellcaster, with a wide range of spells to choose from (including the very tempting Fireball) and the spell slots to use them.

Ritual Adept

Rituals can be powerful spells, and while they take a while to cast, Wizards allow for the use of any spell with the Ritual tag without using a spellslot and without having it prepared, saving those for more in-the-moment abilities, such as 8d6 fire damage in a 20ft radius. No, you do not care how small the room is.

Possible Combinations

Barbarian

Rage makes this combination utterly pointless. You're a full spellcaster with a feature that stops your spellcasting.

Bard

Jack of All Trades and some low-level spells that don't rely on CHA are a nice choice to pick up. It adds some more utility without needing to add them to your spellbook.

Cleric

Heavy armor proficiency, and some first-level spells and cantrips to round out your casting. One level of Cleric is a solid dip choice.

Druid

It's an extra ability score to juggle, but Wild Shape can give you some melee combat options to work with for only 2 levels.

Fighter

A subclass dependent multiclass. As usual, a Fighter dip for Action Surge and some proficiencies is strong, but for Eldritch Knight it can allow yourself some more leeway in your spellcasting, as well as subclass bonuses from Wizard to make it stronger.

Monk

No synergy. Wizard doesn't offer anything to Monk's martial arts, and Monk hampers Wizard's spellcasting.

Paladin

Can do well if you focus on Constitution to have great concentration while buffing yourself, but don't expect anything too crazy outside of it. If you're set on Paladin, Sorcerer is a better option.

Ranger

No real synergy. Splits ability scores for less return than it's worth.

Rogue

Bladesinger wizard combos well for extra AC and spellcasting. If you're feeling funny, use Find Familiar and have it use the Help action for free Advantage whenever you choose.

Sorcerer

Tempting, but redundant. The different stats diminish effectiveness for too similar of features. A dip can be argued for, but there's a certain point of similarity where you just stick with the first class.

Warlock

There are two options to consider here. On the primarily Wizard end of things. with 10 Wizard and 2 Warlock, combining Empowered Invocation and Agonizing Blast can allow you to add both INT and CHA to every Eldritch Blast beam for amazing DpR. Alternatively, you can combine Arcane Ward with Armor of Shadows to gain incredible survivability, and if you pick up Armor of Agathys it can just get silly.

Closing Notes

There are some options that end up redundant, but it's more than plausible where it does fit. Most combinations do better with Wizard as the dip instead of the primary focus, as the focus on spellcasting Wizard forces gets disrupted by most classes.
