The Rogue Class

Like rolling lots of dice? Like never failing anything ever? Rogue is for you.

Notable Features

Sneak Attack

Starts fairly small, but commit to Rogue and you can easily rival Fighter DPS.

Expertise

Look your DM in the eye and ask if a 40 passes. Add your proficiency bonus a second time. This gets very absurd combined with Reliable Talent at 7th level.

Reliable Talent

If you roll below a 10 in a skill you're proficient in, treat the roll as a 10. If you have expertise and a decent score, will save plenty of time not bothering with the dice.

Possible Combinations

Barbarian

A few levels of Barbarian can compliment Rogue well. Reckless Attack creates free advantage opportunities without giving up movement for Steady Aim, and Uncanny Dodge can counteract the risk of it. Unarmored Defense can also work wonders for Rogue, not to mention the Constitution bonus to use it well.

Bard

Never fail a skill check again. Pick up Jack Of All Trades and maybe Swashbuckler to put that Charisma modifier to work. Spellcasting can definitely give Rogue room to work with as well.

Cleric

Nothing Rogue really wants. At most, you want Spellcasting, and there are better options to go about getting it.

Druid

I can see the vision with Wild Shape, but in practice you can probably stealth as straight Rogue better than a fly.

Fighter

Very silly. Take Battle Master and Swashbuckler. See, Sneak Attack only specifies per turn, not per round. One sneak attack on your action, one with Action Surge, and one on reaction with Riposte. Swashbuckler means you don't need Advantage or allies nearby if you're in a 1 on 1. The higher Hit Die and Second Wind can also work wonders for your survivability. Medium Armor could work at lower levels, but your Dexterity should be above 2 very quickly and Medium Armor Master is a poor investement when you want to take Dexterity higher than 3 anyway.

Monk

Tempting, but too much of a commitment. Takes too many levels to be worth. Works better with Monk as the main class and a small dip into Rogue.

Paladin

In theory, a critical hit that will tear your foes asunder singlehandedly. In practice, a Rogue that has to choose to use some of their Sneak Attack dice.

Ranger

A very timeless combo. Hunter's Mark, a Fighting Style, more stealth related abilities with Gloomstalker, and spellcasting to round out the build.

Sorcerer

Some decent options, though it's a tossup whether it's better than sticking with Rogue. In 2014 it was an easy 1-level dip for Draconic Bloodline, but now it takes a while to kick in. Shield and the like make you hard to hit, though.

Warlock

A Hexblade Warlock is an excellent combo for rogues. Take the Devil's Sight incantation to see in magical darkness. The same darkness that you yourself can cast. Free advantage, lots of Sneak Attack, and if you can convince your DM, take Assassin for free critical hits with Assassinate.

Wizard

Bladesinger wizard combos well for extra AC and spellcasting. If you're feeling funny, use Find Familiar and have it use the Help action for free Advantage whenever you choose.

Closing Notes

There are some builds that the Rogue doesn't want, but there are plenty more that can work well. Several classes either cover Rogue's weaknesses or augment its strengths.
