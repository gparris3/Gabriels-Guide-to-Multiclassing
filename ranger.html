An in-between of three things this time: Fighter's combat, Druid's nature-based casting, and Rogue's skills.

Notable Features

Favored Enemy

Hunter's Mark is a useful spell, and you can cast it for free twice (or more depending on your level) per long rest. It's always prepared as well, freeing up your chosen spells for other options as well.

Spellcasting

Ranger has some good exclusive spells, but even aside from those more slots to work with is always a helpful addition.

Possible Combinations

Barbarian

I can see the vision roleplay-wise, but in practice some features clash, like Rage restricting your spellcasting.

Bard

No real synnergies here. Lots of utility, I suppose.

Cleric

Primarily Ranger. Take Archery. Bless will give you a bonus to hit along with supporting others, as well as supporting your Constitution saves. Certain combinations like Hunter ranger and War cleric can get several attacks early on.

Druid

Redundant. If inspiration strikes, Gloomstalker ranger and Spores druid could have potential, but Druid doesn't multiclass well thanks to Wild Shape. Just don't use Circle of the Moon.

Fighter

An easy way to shift Ranger into a more melee-focused build. Heavy armor, more proficiencies and weapon masteries,

Monk

Unarmored defense is good, and the extra Wisdom to get use out of Focus and Unarmored Defense will also boost your casting. An unusual direction to take Ranger in, but an interesting one.

Paladin

Two half-casters unfortunately do not make a full martial and full caster. Prerequisites spread your stats thin, but if you make it past those then their subclasses can synergize well, and both of their targeting features can combine for a powerful melee turn.

Rogue

A very timeless combo. Hunter's Mark, a Fighting Style, more stealth related abilities with Gloomstalker, and spellcasting to round out some of Rogue's weak points.

Sorcerer

Very little synergy here, with Ranger focusing on DEX and WIS while Sorcerers focus on CHA your scores spread thin. 2014 Hunter Ranger's Horde Breaker used to provide some synergy to work with melee spells, but 2024 reworded this to no longer work.

Warlock

There is potential here, but it does require a specific goal in mind. Darkness and Devil's Sight can synergize well with Gloom Stalker Ranger, and a lot of Ranger's spells don't really need WIS. It has its niche, if you're set on the combo.

Wizard

No real synergy. Splits ability scores for less return than it's worth.

Closing Notes

While it doesn't apply everywhere, when Ranger does synergize with a class it provides useful skills to round out some more focused classes.

by Gabriel Parrish
